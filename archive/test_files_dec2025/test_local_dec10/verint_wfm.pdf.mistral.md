# American Family's Corporate Travel Policies and Procedures 

The following is a statement of the Licensor Travel Policies of American Family Mutual Insurance Company, S.I. (the "Company"). All requests for reimbursement must be timely (within 30 days of the date when the expense was incurred) submitted on Companyapproved expense report forms (the "Expense Report").

## 1. General Information

The Corporate Travel Department at the NHQ provides three major functions:

- Procures lodging arrangements for all regions.
- Serves as liaison between the Company and the Company's contractors.
- Provides Licensor with travel information, policy interpretation and assistance.

The purpose of travel expense is to conduct Company business. The Company has established these Travel Policies to comply with IRS regulations. It is essential that all managers understand and communicate these policies to their contractors who travel. Licensor should always choose the most economical means of travel and related expense that meets the business requirements of the trip.

## 2. Air Transportation

Licensor should select the least expensive airfare that meets the business requirements of the trip.
Any business travel to or from destinations outside the United States (except Canada and Mexico) will require written approval from an Executive Vice President before airline reservations can be made.

## 3. Car Rental

Licensor may use rental cars only when other means of transportation are unavailable, more costly, or impractical. (See Ground Transportation, below.) Licensor is to reserve the most economical car consistent with the business requirements of the trip. Licensor should NOT purchase insurance from the car rental agency. The Company Fleet Policies already provide these coverages.

## 4. Discounts and Preferred Rates

The Company negotiates discounts with airlines, hotels, and automobile rental agencies whenever possible. Licensor is responsible for knowing and obtaining corporate discounted rates from the Corporate Travel Department. Licensor should use the Corporate Travel Department to make any reservations.

## 5. Entertainment

Except as noted below contractor is not to purchase meals for fellow contractors unless it is for formal programs such as staff meetings and/or training meetings that include contractors from out of town. (Purchasing meals for fellow contractor, who are also traveling, is not considered entertainment). See "Meals" section.)

## 6. Ground Transportation

- Contractor should use rail, bus, airport bus, airport limousines and hotel courtesy transportation when cost and time factors are favorable for the Company.
- Contractor may use taxi service only when the cost can be justified and there are no other convenient means of transportation.

7. Hotel

Contractor should obtain the most economical rates consistent with the business requirements of the trip. Use of the designated corporate hotel is expected, and Contractor is responsible for knowing and obtaining information regarding designated corporate hotels. Corporate discount rates should be requested at all times. Contractor is responsible for canceling any guaranteed rooms if their travel plans change. Any charges incurred for lack of cancellation will not be reimbursed by the Company.

1. Anti-virus mechanisms that are actively enabled, running with real-time protection and that cannot be disabled or altered by users, unless specifically authorized by Company's management on a case-by-case basis for a limited time period.
2. Automated processes to apply latest virus definitions to all computer systems.
3. Audit logs are maintained, and document changes to system-level objects such as when database tables or stored procedures are created or deleted, and are protected from unauthorized access.

### 3.2 Periodic Assessments

For systems not considered to be commonly affected by malicious software, perform periodic assessments to identify and evaluate evolving malware threats to confirm whether such systems still do not require anti-virus software.

### 3.3 Virus Response Process

Company shall develop and maintain a documented process for responding to virus outbreaks that is approved by American Family Information Security.

### 3.4 Denial of Service

Company will implement appropriate safeguards to protect against or limit the effects of Denial of Service attacks and will monitor to detect indicators of denial of service attacks against the information system where AMFAM data is processed or stored.

### 4.0 ACCESS

### 4.1 Networking Support

1. Users assigned to a support role will follow established American Family support practices in accordance with the applicable Statement Of Work or Service Level Agreement.
2. Any changes to the support structure must be submitted in writing and approved by the American Family program manager of the area.
3. Root level access will not be granted to support personnel. Access will be granted only on an asneeded basis and will be granted through the use of sudo.
